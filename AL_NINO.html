AL - NINO

El Niño is a naturally occurring climate phenomenon characterized by warmer-than-average sea surface temperatures in the central and eastern tropical Pacific Ocean. It occurs irregularly every 2 to 7 years and alters global weather patterns, frequently causing extreme conditions like severe droughts in some regions and heavy rainfall in others


How it Works ?
Normally, strong trade winds blow east-to-west along the equator, pushing warm surface water toward Asia and Australia. During an El Niño, these winds weaken or reverse, allowing the warm water pool to spread eastward toward the coast of South America. This shift in warm water changes atmospheric pressure and disrupts the Pacific jet stream, which triggers a domino effect on global climate


Global Impacts
Because El Niño alters massive air and ocean currents, its effects are felt worldwide.

  • India & Asia: Weakens the monsoon winds, often leading to suppressed rainfall, dry spells, and intense heatwaves.
  • North America: Tends to create warmer, drier winters in the North, and wetter, cooler conditions in the South and Southeast.
  • South America & Australia: Brings heavy rain and increased flooding to Peru and Ecuador, while causing severe droughts and bushfire risks in Australia and Indonesia.
  • Global Temperatures By releasing massive amounts of heat from the Pacific into the atmosphere, El Niño often drives up global average temperatures
